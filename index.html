Nothing is as it zeems

An art collaboration between Mahima Singh and Zeel Sanghvi

Every week a python script produces a randomly generated theme that we visualise individually. We are free to interpret the theme as we like and present it how we want, leaving room for experimentation and creativity. We also included writeups with our designs explaining our thought process.

This week's theme is 'no grass'

no grass

Mahima

For this week I wanted to so another data art piece. Initially, I was going to visualize marijuana legalisation per country but something didn't seem right. I moved to Canada in the middle of winter and when I landed, there was no grass. I knew I had to make something around that physical shift. I had boarded a plane in New Delhi, a city lush with greenery and landed in Canada, a country where all the trees were covered in snow.

I think this piece is pretty cool because if you turn it upside down it says one thing and when you flip it says another, but the tree remains the same, somewhat like my journey around the world.

most dry

Mahima

After stumbling upon Mona Chalabi on Instagram, I have been fascinated with data art. I took the chance with "Most dry" to explore some of that on my own. So I set out looking for data to present dryness/ wetness and I found rainfall data in India. Because this was a quick turnaround piece I didn't add more datasets as dimensions to the piece.

Each coloured drop represents a month from the legend. The heavier the drop ( the closer it is to the ground) the more rainfall that month. The intensity of rain shafts represents the annual rainfall that year. The darker the shaft, the more rainfall that year. 2009 was the "most dry" year from the data set.

To get the basic positions of the drops, I charted a dot plot from the data. I then flipped it ( to get the heavier dots closer to the bottom) and drew drops on the positions of the dots.

For this week's theme, I didn't want to make a lateral visual about a shadow falling. Though I did sketch out a few of those possibilities.

For this piece, I was looking through Unsplash images for inspiration and came across the picture of the stairs going down. Looking at the picture, I felt like I was falling too. Waiting on art

Methodology

We first met while covering the 2019 general elections in India for the BBC in New Delhi. After learning about our shared appreciation for art and design we started collaborating weekly to keep our creative juices flowing. We would write down random words on paper and pick two from the ever-growing bunch every week. We'd then create art pieces around the "Theme of the week" and showcase them to each other. As our contracts with the BBC ended and we moved on to different jobs this personal collaborative project also took on different forms. The latest is what you see on this web page.

Now instead of picking the theme from a set of chits, we have a python script do it for us. The code takes two words at random from a google sheet that we regularly update with words and phrases and creates a theme.
